Dr. Jiban Podder

Professor

Jiban Podder is a Professor of Physics, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), Dhaka, Bangladesh where he has been teaching since 1st March 1989. Podder was born in Narayangang, Bangladesh on 24th December 1959. He received his B.Sc. (Honours) and M.Sc. in Physics from Dhaka University in 1980 and 1981 respectively. Podder received his M.Phil in 1987 from BUET and Ph.D in Physics from BUET in 1995 under the research collaboration of Anna University, India. Podder joined at the Bangladesh Atomic Energy Commission as a Scientific Officer in June 1987. He was appointed as a lecturer in the Department of Physics, BUET in 1989. He became an Assistant Professor in 1992, Associate Professor in 1998 and full Professor in 2002. Dr. Podder pursued Postdoctoral research at Anna University, India, during 2000-2001 and Nagoya Institute of Technology, Japan under JSPS fellowship during 2003-2005. Prof. Podder served the Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering, University of Saskatchewan, Canada as Visiting Professor from 2012 to 2015. Dr. Podder’s area of research interests include growth kinetics, Non-linear optical crystals, compound semiconductor thin films, nano-materials, carbon and carbonaceous materials for optoelectronic and photonic applications.

Dr. Podder has supervised so far 20 M.Sc, 30 M. Phil. and 6 Ph.D students. Prof. Podder has evaluated over 250 Ph.D theses from different Universities around the world. As of 2024, Prof. Podder has published over 195 research articles in refereed journals & proceedings and author of ‘Applied Engineering Physics: Electricity and Magnetism’ for Science and Engineering students and one U. S Patent Apl. No. 61/932662‘Methods and Apparatus for Crystallization of Salts’.Prof. Podder is a Fellow of Bangladesh Academy of Sciences, Bangladesh Physical Society and now acting as an Editor-In-Chief of the Bangladesh Journal of Scientific Research. Currently, Prof. Podder is working as a Visiting Professor in the Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering, University of Saskatchewan, Canada on sabbatical from October'2021 to September'2022. Prof. Podder was acting as Dean of the Faculty of Science, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology from November 18, 2022 to November 17, 2024.

Effect of silver doping on the band gap tuning of tungsten oxide thin films for optoelectronic applications

  • Md. Arifuzzaman, Tusar Saha, Jiban Podder, Fahad Al-Bin, Hari Narayan Das
  • Journal Paper
  • Heliyon, 10, e27761, 2024

In the cutting-edge world, semiconductor metal oxides usually tend to have a high optical band gap (>3.0 eV), significantly acceptable for potential optoelectronic applications. The present study discusses the synthesize of pristine tungsten trioxide (WO3) and Silver (Ag) doped WO3 (Ag: WO3) thin films onto a glass substrate at 450 ◦C, with varying concentrations of Ag doping (2, 4, 6, 8 and 10 at.%) using a simple Spray Pyrolysis Technique.

Dr. Podder’s area of research includes:

1) Research interests are focused in exploring and understanding the physical properties of functional electronic materials and low dimensional nano-structured materials of wide band gap oxide semiconductors for transparent electronic, energy-efficiency-related prospective optoelectronic devices and different sensor technologies. Research is also dedicated to the development and application of first-principles modeling tools for the theory and simulation of materials.

2) Nucleation and growth kinetics, crystallization of mineral salts, potential organic & inorganic single crystals for nonlinear optical applications. Potential recovery of potash from cooling pond, qualitative and quantitative study of minerals identification by spectral imaging techniques.

Research Contributions:

Developed a Crystal Growth Laboratory:

Crystallization of single crystal has been developed as an important field over the many years, both for basic research and industrial applications in many scientific disciplines. The rapid development of optical communication system has led to a demand for nonlinear optical materials for high performance in electronics, optoelectronics devices. Considering the technological point of view, a crystal growth laboratory has been developed in the Department of Physics, BUET since 1995. Both organic and inorganic, semi-organic singles crystals have been grown in the laboratory from low temperature solution growth technique. Four Ph.D, 10 M.Phil and 6 M.Sc degrees have been awarded from this laboratory and students are using this laboratory for growing some novel potential single crystals.

Developed a Low Cost Spray Pyrolysis Laboratory for Thin Film Deposition:

An inexpensive and a low cost spray pyrolysis laboratory have been developed for thin film deposition in the Department of Physics, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology, Dhaka. A series of high quality transparent metal oxides thin films of ZnO, CuO, SnO2, MnO, MnO2, Mn2O3, Mn3O4, NiO, WO3, In2O3, CdO, Fe2O3, Co3O4 have been synthesized on a plain glass substrate via. a thermal spray pyrolysis technique. This research aims at developing a low cost and an efficient gas sensing device using nanostructured materials as building blocks for both the active layers and the transparent electrode contacts. Using this facility three students have already been completed Ph.D thesis work and 20 students completed M.Phil and 10 M.Sc degrees.

Developed a Neutron Radiography Facility:

Installation and production of Neutron radiography set up consists of a Collimator, Beam catcher, Beam shutter, Sample table and shielding wall using TRIGA-Mark-II research reactor (3MW), preparation of gamma and neutrons shielding materials and their characterizations have been done during the service period of Atomic Energy Commission under the Reactor and Neutron Physics Division at the Institute of Nuclear Science and Technology, AERE, Savar.
